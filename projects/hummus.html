Project Hummus will be an iOS app with a suite of features that helps anyone eating at USC dining halls have the best meal possible.

This idea is based on a prototype I created with three other team members during my first hackathon in Fall 2019 — TrojanHacks.

First, the app will display the menus of the three dining halls at USC. This display will be improved over USC's menu display in several ways: it will show the foods in order of importance (entrees and main dishes before condiments, for example); and it will be customized to your food preferences, allergies, etc.

Food preferences will be an important part of the app. You can get notified when a food you've favorited is on the menu. In the future, a machine learning algorithm could help you discover new dishes you'll enjoy.

Second, given these food preferences, the app can give a recommendation for which dining hall you'll enjoy eating at most based on how good you'll like the food there. This can also be improved with a future ML algorithm.

Third, a community-type feature of rating entrees on a given day can give alerts to other users about, say abnormally good ramen at the USC Village or the pizza at Parkside. Simultaneously, over time, every entree at the dining halls will have an average rating for a more informed choice when you're about to go out for dinner!
